--- DIETARY TOXINS (DOGS & CATS) ---

- Grapes and raisins cause kidney failure in dogs and should never be fed.

- Chocolate contains theobromine which is highly toxic to dogs and cats.

- Onions, garlic, and chives can cause red blood cell damage and anemia in pets.

- Xylitol (a sweetener found in sugar-free gum and some peanut butters) is deadly to dogs.

- Macadamia nuts cause weakness, vomiting, and tremors in dogs.

- Avocados contain persin, which is toxic to birds and some other small pets.

- Alcohol and caffeine are strictly prohibited and can cause heart failure or seizures.

- Cooked bones are dangerous because they can splinter and cause internal punctures.

- Raw yeast dough can expand in a pet's stomach, causing severe pain and bloat.



--- HOUSEHOLD & PLANT HAZARDS ---

- Lilies are extremely toxic to cats; even a small amount of pollen can cause kidney failure.

- Azaleas, sago palms, and tulips are poisonous if ingested by pets.

- Household cleaners, bleach, and laundry pods are toxic and must be kept out of reach.

- Antifreeze (ethylene glycol) has a sweet taste but is fatal even in tiny amounts.

- Human medications (like Ibuprofen or Tylenol) are highly toxic to pets unless prescribed by a vet.



--- GENERAL SAFETY & CARE ---

- Always provide access to fresh, clean water throughout the day.

- Pets should be kept in a climate-controlled environment to prevent heatstroke or hypothermia.

- Collars should be fitted properly to prevent choking or getting caught on furniture.

- Exercise should be appropriate for the pet's age, breed, and health status.

- Regular flea, tick, and heartworm prevention is necessary for outdoor safety.

- Electrical cords should be covered or tucked away to prevent chewing and electrocution.

- Small toys or objects that can be swallowed pose a major choking and obstruction hazard.
